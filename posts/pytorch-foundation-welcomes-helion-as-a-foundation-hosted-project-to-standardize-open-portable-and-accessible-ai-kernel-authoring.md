# PyTorch Foundation Welcomes Helion as a Foundation-Hosted Project to Standardize Open, Portable, and Accessible AI Kernel Authoring

**Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Author:** PyTorch Foundation  
**Category:** Announcements

## Overview

The PyTorch Foundation has announced that [Helion](https://helionlang.com/index.html) has joined as its newest foundation-hosted project alongside DeepSpeed, PyTorch, Ray, and vLLM. This contribution by Meta addresses a critical layer of the AI stack, making kernel authoring a first-class part of PyTorch by strengthening custom kernel creation and reducing manual coding effort through autotuning.

## Key Highlights

Helion joins the Foundation as AI model development expands from training to an inference boom, elevating the importance of serving models at scale. In this landscape, where hardware, software, and model architectures are shifting simultaneously, engineering teams face significant hurdles in cross-platform compatibility.

Helion eliminates bottlenecks associated with model architectures and execution, providing developers with:
- Radically simpler kernels
- Automated ahead-of-time autotuning
- Greater hardware performance portability

## What is Helion?

Helion is a Python-embedded domain-specific language (DSL) for authoring machine learning kernels, designed to compile down to multiple backends for hardware heterogeneity (Triton, TileIR, and more coming soon). Helion aims to raise the level of abstraction compared to kernel languages, making it easier to write efficient kernels while enabling more automation in the autotuning process.

## ExecuTorch Integration

In addition to Helion joining the Foundation, ExecuTorch is becoming part of PyTorch Core. Started at Meta, ExecuTorch continues to extend PyTorch model functionality for on-edge and on-device environments under the Foundation, ensuring that ecosystem and technical decisions are made in an open, community-guided manner.

## About the PyTorch Foundation

The PyTorch Foundation is a community-driven hub supporting the open source PyTorch framework and a broader portfolio of innovative open source AI projects, including DeepSpeed, Helion, PyTorch, Ray, and vLLM. Hosted by the [Linux Foundation](https://hubs.la/Q03PC25n0), the PyTorch Foundation provides a vendor-neutral, trusted home for collaborative development in the open AI ecosystem.
